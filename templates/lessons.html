Saxophone Lessons


The saxophone is a highly versatile instrument, it has been seen in nearly every genre of modern music (post 1900) and has a wide range of skills. In these lessons, we will be focusing on two vast genres; classical and jazz. In these genres, we will look at the techniques involved, history, various performers, music theory, and performance etiquette.

In addition to those skills, we will also be working on good practice room habits, such as how to warm up, keeping a personal practice log, taking care of the instrument, etc. It is my goal for each lesson that my student will have something to take home and that they will have fun!


Composition Lessons


Composing is an amazing means of expressing yourself! There is a growing need for composers in the current musical climate as more shows, movies, musicals, podcasts, and more are being made. Channel your creativity to be a part of it!

In your lessons, the primary focus will be finding your voice! The idea of ‘Finding Your Voice’ is a really abstract journey that takes a lot of introspection. We will explore music you like, and equally important, don’t enjoy, to further understand your tastes so you can develop your style!

Several skills you will develop:

    Writing music by hand

    Notation softwares (Sibelius/Dorico/Notion/Finale)

    DAW softwares (StudioOne/Ableton Live/Pro Tools/Garage Band/Logic Pro/Cubase)

    Music theory

    Score analysis

    Orchestration
